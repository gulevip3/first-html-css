Mihajlo Pupin



On this site you can find out everything about the works of Mihajlo Pupin, a prominent Serbian scientist, inventor and writer. His most famous book, From Settler to Inventor (1923), provides an autobiographical account of his life and career, for which he received the Pulitzer Prize in 1924. Apart from this book, Pupin also wrote "The New Reformation: From Physical to Spiritual Reality" (1927) and "Romance about the Machine" (1930), which deal with his scientific ideas and philosophical thoughts. Pupin's works cover a wide range of subjects, including telecommunications, electrical engineering and the social aspects of science. His literary activity was not limited to autobiography; he also wrote scientific papers that significantly contributed to the development of various fields. Details of all his books, as well as information about his life and work, are available on the site. Pupin was active in promoting Serbian culture and education, founding several foundations that supported young talents. His contribution to science and literature left an indelible mark, and his work is still studied today. For all information about Pupin's works, research and impact on the world of science, this site is a comprehensive resource.
